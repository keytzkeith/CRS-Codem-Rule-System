For a personal trade dashboard and journal, the mistake most people make is trying to build a full prop-firm analytics platform on day one. You do not need that. You need a dashboard that answers:

Am I following my setup?

Am I improving?

Where am I losing money?

What patterns are working?

Am I being disciplined?

What you actually need
1. Core dashboard stats

These should be the first things you see:

Total trades

Win rate

Net PnL

Average win

Average loss

Risk-to-reward average

Profit factor

Best day

Worst day

Current streak

Weekly performance

Monthly performance

That alone already gives you a strong overview.

2. Trade list / history

A clean table with:

Date

Pair

Direction

Setup type

Session

Entry

Stop loss

Take profit

Result in R

Result in money

Status: win / loss / breakeven

Screenshot link or image

Notes

This is the backbone. Everything else can be calculated from this.

3. Journal fields

This is where the real growth happens. Keep it short so you will actually fill it in.

For each trade, journal:

Why I took the trade

HTF bias

Entry model used

Was it according to plan? yes/no

Emotion before trade

Emotion after trade

Mistake made

Lesson learned

Optional:

Confidence rating

Sleep / focus rating

News nearby yes/no

Do not add too many fields or you’ll stop journaling.

4. Setup tagging

This is very important for your style.

You should be able to tag trades with:

Reversal

Continuation

Liquidity sweep

BOS entry

OB retest

HTF 4H confirmation

M15 confirmation

London session

New York session

Counter-trend

A+ setup

FOMO / invalid

This helps you later answer:
“Which setup actually makes me money?”

5. Charts / visuals

Only add visuals that help decision-making.

Essentials:

Equity curve

PnL by week or month

Win rate by setup

Performance by pair

Performance by session

Distribution of wins / losses

Calendar heatmap

Mistake frequency chart

That is enough. Avoid fancy charts that look cool but say nothing.

6. Rule tracking

Since your style is rule-based, add a small discipline section:

For each trade:

HTF BOS confirmed? yes/no

Pulled back to OB? yes/no

M15 confirmation present? yes/no

In direction of bias? yes/no

Session valid? yes/no

Minimum RR met? yes/no

Then show a dashboard card like:

Rule-followed trades: 82%

Trades taken outside plan: 18%

This may become one of your most useful stats.

Best structure for your CRS dashboard

Keep it simple with these pages:

1. Dashboard

Overview cards + main charts

2. Trades

All trades in a table with filters

3. Journal

Single trade detailed view with notes and screenshots

4. Analytics

Breakdowns by setup, pair, session, month, rule-following

5. Settings

Theme, account size, risk per trade, imported data preferences

That is enough for V1.

Recommended design style for CRS

Since you want it to feel cool but not overcomplicated:

Dark theme

Clean cards

Accent color based on your theme

Rounded corners

Soft shadows or glass feel, but subtle

Minimal sidebar

Table with colored status chips

Performance colors:

green for wins

red for losses

amber for breakeven / warning

Large clean stat cards

Simple modern charts

Think:
trader journal + portfolio dashboard + calm minimal UI

What to avoid building right now

Do not spend time building:

Social features

Too many broker integrations

AI coach

Complex authentication roles

Multi-user system

Advanced tax reporting

20+ chart widgets

Too many journal questions

Strategy backtester inside the dashboard

Build the thing you will actually use daily.
